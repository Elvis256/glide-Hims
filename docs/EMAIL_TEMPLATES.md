## EMAIL BEST PRACTICES FOR HEALTHCARE SALES

### Best Times to Email

- **Early morning (6:30–7:30 AM):** Before ward rounds begin — administrators often check email during this window.
- **After 5:00 PM:** When clinical duties wind down and admin work begins.
- **Avoid:** Mid-morning (8–11 AM) when rounds, meetings, and patient flow are at peak.
- **Best days:** Tuesday through Thursday. Mondays are hectic; Fridays lose momentum.

### Subject Line Tips

- Keep subject lines under 8 words when possible.
- Lead with their facility name or a specific pain point — not your product name.
- Use numbers when you can ("Cut HMIS reporting time by 70%").
- Avoid all-caps, exclamation marks, and salesy language ("FREE", "ACT NOW").

### Personalization Tips

- Reference their facility by name — always.
- Mention a specific challenge relevant to their facility type (referral hospital vs. health centre).
- If you met them at a conference or were referred, say so in the first line.
- Reference recent MOH directives or HMIS reporting deadlines when relevant.

### What NOT to Do

- **Do not spam.** If they haven't responded after the full sequence, wait 60+ days before re-engaging.
- **Do not discuss patient data casually.** Never imply you will access, migrate, or view their patient records without proper data handling agreements.
- **Do not pressure.** Healthcare administrators carry life-and-death responsibilities — respect their time and decision pace.
- **Do not badmouth their current system.** Position Glide HIMS as an upgrade, not a criticism of how they work today.
- **Do not send attachments in the first email.** They may get flagged or ignored. Use attachments only after initial engagement.
